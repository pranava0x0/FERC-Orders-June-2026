COMMENTS OF SMA 
 
The SMA Group welcomes the opportunity to comment on the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission’s 
(FERC) Advance Notice of Proposed Rulemaking (ANOPR) regarding the large-load interconnections.1 By 
initiating this timely ANOPR, FERC is taking important steps to improve the efficiency and prioritization of 
the interconnection process for large loads like AI data centers. SMA believes that without significant 
reforms, the existing system will be unable to support the rapid deployment of AI data centers necessary 
for the U.S. to win the global race in AI and digital infrastructure, while ensuring that the regular rate payers 
have access to affordable and reliable power. 
 
The advanced, high-performing, grid-forming inverters produced by SMA alongside battery energy storage 
systems (BESS) can play a central role in fulfilling the objectives of the ANOPR. SMA strongly recommends 
and requests that, as the Commission develops a Notice of Proposed Rulemaking and a final rule, it ensures 
that grid operators and other stakeholders can fully utilize this essential, adaptable, and high-performing 
resource. Advanced grid-forming inverters with BESS can reduce the need for costly infrastructure 
expenditures, which would otherwise be borne by consumers. The capabilities of these systems are versatile; 
they provide system stability to the grid including provision of inertia, short circuit level, system 
restoration/black start capability, fast frequency reserves and grid resilience through power quality 
assurance – basically, everything a rotating turbine provides.

About SMA 
 
SMA has been actively engaged in grid interconnection processes in the U.S. and globally for decades, 
collaborating with utilities, regulators, and industry stakeholders to develop and implement efficient, 
reliable, and standardized interconnection practices. Advanced inverters play a key role in managing the 
interaction between the power grid and large electrical loads through power electronics. SMA’s portfolio 
includes grid-forming inverters and control technology for BESS that enhance power system stability, provide 
pro-active voltage and frequency support at critical grid events, as well as enable rapid response to 
fluctuating loads. SMA’s technologies are deployed across a wide range of applications, including utility-
scale energy projects and critical infrastructure.2 The first ever stability plant, called Blackhillock, is located 
in the United Kingdom. It delivers 370 MWs of inertia and 116MVA of Short Circuit level – like a gas 
turbine plant – and utilizes SMA’s grid-forming inverters.  
 
SMA has an installed inverter capacity of over 160 GW across more than 200 countries, including more 
than 45 GW in the U.S. alone. Headquartered in Germany with a branch office since 2008 in Rocklin, 
California, SMA employes 3,900 individuals in 19 countries, stimulating local economies through high-
paying manufacturing jobs and collaborations with regional suppliers and service providers. SMA has an 
integration partner for the assembly of medium voltage transformers and switch gears in Little Rock, 
Arkansas for the SMA Medium Voltage Power Stations. SMA’s large-scale string-inverter line has a 
manufacturing partner in Nashville, Tennessee, where PEAK3 is assembled. SMA is committed to 
expanding U.S. production and supply chains to support critical infrastructure needs.

ANOPR and Consumer Affordability 
 
SMA supports the ANOPR objective to ensure that U.S. consumers and industrial customers, including 
strategically important loads such as data centers “have access to affordable, reliable, and secure energy.”3 
The ANOPR, including by incorporating SMA’s recommendations, can significantly advance this goal: 
 
 The ANOPR’s Seventh Principle, by enabling large loads to curtail their demand from the grid 
provides the opportunity to reduce costs by accelerating interconnections and avoiding expensive 
infrastructure investments.4

Conclusion  
 
SMA appreciates the Department of Energy and the Commission’s leadership in addressing the challenges 
of large load interconnection. SMA strongly supports FERC’s continued efforts to develop a rulemaking that 
incorporates the principles outlined in the ANOPR, while ensuring that advanced technologies like grid-
forming inverters and BESS are recognized for their role in supporting process expedition while ensuring a 
reliable and resilient power system. SMA looks forward to continued engagement with the Commission and 
stakeholders on this critical issue.
