COMMENTS OF ONCOR ELECTRIC DELIVERY COMPANY LLC 
 
Pursuant to the notice inviting comments issued by the Federal Energy Regulatory 
Commission (“FERC” or the “Commission”) on October 27, 2025 in the above-captioned 
docket and the November 7, 2025 notice extending the comment deadlines, Oncor Electric 
Delivery Company LLC (“Oncor”) provides the following comments on the advanced 
notice of proposed rulemaking (“ANOPR”) regarding large load interconnection proposed 
by Secretary of Energy Chris Wright on October 23, 2025. 
As an electric transmission and distribution utility that operates solely within Texas 
and is not a “public utility” under section 201(e) of the Federal Power Act (“FPA”) subject 
to plenary FERC jurisdiction, Oncor takes no position at this time on the merits of the 
reforms proposed in the ANOPR or the Commission’s legal authority to implement such 
reforms under FPA sections 201, 205, and 206.  However, Oncor encourages the 
Commission to make clear that any rule on large load interconnection it issues in this 
proceeding does not apply to entities like the Electric Reliability Council of Texas, Inc. 
(“ERCOT”) and market participants operating solely within ERCOT that are not “public 
utilities” under FPA section 201(e).  In any rule issued in this proceeding, the Commission 
should carefully define key terms and avoid unnecessary references to provisions like FPA 
section 215 that apply to entities that are not jurisdictional “public utilities” and thus could
