REPLY COMMENTS OF 
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN 
 
Pursuant to the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission’s (“FERC”) November 7, 2025 
Notice Granting Extension of Time, Michigan Attorney General Dana Nessel (the “Attorney 
General”), on behalf of the people of the State of Michigan respectfully submits these Reply 
Comments in response to initial comments submitted by interested stakeholders that address the 
October 27, 2025 Notice Inviting Comments regarding the Advanced Notice of Proposed 
Rulemaking (“ANOPR”) proposed by the Secretary of Energy on October 23, 2025.   
The Attorney General replies to present support for several points raised in comments by 
the National Association of State Utility Consumer Advocates (“NASUCA”). The Attorney 
General replies to express support for NASUCA’s comments as to jurisdictional concerns 
presented by the ANOPR’s arguments and potential impact.1 The Attorney General further replies 
to express support for NASUCA’s comments as to why expedited action would be inappropriate 
here.2 
The Attorney General also replies to express support for the request raised in comment by 
a group of several states’ attorneys general and ratepayer advocates for FERC to “meaningfully
