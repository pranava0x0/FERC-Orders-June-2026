Dear Secretary Reese:
Maven Solutions respectfully submits the attached research brief, The Community-Development Gap in
FERC Docket RM26-4-000, for inclusion in the public record of the above-captioned proceeding. This
filing is submitted in response to the Commission's October 27, 2025 Notice Inviting Comments and in
anticipation of the Commission's forthcoming action, as announced in its April 16, 2026 Order
Regarding Intent to Act.
Maven Solutions is an independent consulting practice specializing in technical program management,
AI infrastructure governance, and community-impact assessment for large-scale technology
deployments. The undersigned has over fifteen years of experience in program management across
enterprise technology, merger-and-acquisition integration, and AI platform operations.
The attached brief identifies a structural gap in the docket record. After reviewing the principal
categories of comments filed by the approximately 175 organizations that participated in the initial and
reply comment periods, Maven Solutions found that no commenter substantively addressed the
community-infrastructure dimensions of large-load interconnection — specifically, the workforce
housing, education, healthcare, water system, and transportation infrastructure required to sustain host
communities that absorb facilities consuming electrical capacity equivalent to mid-sized cities.
The brief examines this gap through the lens of Wyoming, where proposed data center developments
would, if fully constructed, exceed the state's total existing electrical generation capacity of
approximately 10,200 megawatts. Wyoming's Public Service Commission — which oversees what may
be the most disproportionately affected regulatory jurisdiction in the nation relative to existing
generation baseline — did not file comments in this docket. The brief documents this absence and
presents a ten-dimension community-development evaluation standard designed to assess
host-community readiness across the full range of infrastructure systems that determine whether
large-load facilities remain viable over multi-decade operational lifecycles.
Maven Solutions offers this research contribution in the interest of ensuring that the Commission's
forthcoming regulatory framework accounts for the complete set of infrastructure conditions that
determine whether large-load interconnection is not only technically feasible and legally durable, but
