Re: Comments of Brian Handshy in Docket No. RM26-4-000
Large Generator Interconnection Agreements and Procedures
Dear Secretary Bose:
Pursuant to the Notice of Proposed Rulemaking issued by the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission in the
above-referenced docket, Brian Handshy, Principal Consultant at Front Door Technologies LLC, respectfully
submits the enclosed comments addressing proposals to use Virtual Power Plant resources and residential
Demand Response for data center interconnection offsets.
This comment demonstrates through rigorous quantitative analysis that applying regional Resource Adequacy
metrics (specifically Effective Load Carrying Capability values) to local interconnection engineering creates
systematic reliability risks due to fundamental differences between these two grid reliability mechanisms. Using
Monte Carlo simulation with empirically grounded probability distributions, the analysis reveals that Virtual
Power Plant portfolios provide less than 5% of their regional capacity credit value for local interconnection
purposes—representing order-of-magnitude overestimation if regional metrics are applied directly to
interconnection studies.
The comment concludes that while Virtual Power Plants provide valuable contributions to regional Resource
Adequacy and should continue to receive appropriate compensation through capacity markets, their statistical
performance across multi-state footprints cannot substitute for the deterministic, worst-case physical analysis
required by the Commission's interconnection study standards under Order No. 2003 and Order No. 2023.
Please do not hesitate to contact me if you require any additional information or clarification regarding these
comments.
