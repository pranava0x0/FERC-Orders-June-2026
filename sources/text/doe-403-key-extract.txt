== Cover letter (p.1–2) ==
- The Secretary of Energy, Washington DC, October 23, 2025.
- Addressed to: Chairman David Rosner; Commissioners Laura Swett, Lindsay S. See, Judy W. Chang.
- Re: Secretary's direction that FERC initiate rulemaking re: interconnection of large loads,
  pursuant to Section 403 of the Department of Energy Organization Act.
- "Historically, the Commission has not exerted jurisdiction over load interconnections. It is my
  view that the interconnection of large loads directly to the interstate transmission system ...
  falls squarely within the Commission's jurisdiction." (16 U.S.C. § 824)
- Pursuant to §403 authority (42 U.S.C. § 7173(a)), attaches an ANOPR for FERC's consideration.
- "I look forward to your consideration and final action (no later than April 30, 2026)."
- Signed: Chris Wright, Secretary of Energy.
- Note: NEPA does not apply to §403(a) proposals (DOE NEPA Implementing Procedures §2.1(c)(8)).

== ANOPR — "Ensuring the Timely and Orderly Interconnection of Large Loads" ==
- Large loads defined as > 20 MW, consistent with Order No. 2003 large generation resources.
- Background: NERC — demand growth higher than at any point in past two decades, driven largely by
  data centers; size/speed of data-center connection presents unique forecasting/planning challenges.
- Jurisdiction (4 legal justifications): (1) like generator interconnections, large-load
  interconnections are a "critical component of open access transmission service"; (2) a practice
  directly affecting jurisdictional wholesale rates; (3) does not impinge on States' authority over
  retail sales or generation siting; (4) contrary view conflicts with the FPA's core purposes.
  Anchored in Order No. 888, New York v. FERC (535 U.S. 1), Order No. 1920.
- Generator-interconnection lineage: Order No. 2003 (Large Generator, >20 MW), Order No. 845 (2018),
  Order No. 2023 (2023 — cluster study, study deposits, readiness, withdrawal/study-delay penalties).

== The 14 Principles for Reform (paraphrased from p.10–14) ==
 1. Limit FERC jurisdiction to interconnections directly to transmission facilities (seven-factor test).
 2. Reforms apply only to new loads > 20 MW (and hybrids where load > 20 MW); comment sought on threshold.
 3. To the extent practicable, study load and hybrid facilities together with generating facilities
    (efficient siting; minimize network upgrades).
 4. Like generators, load/hybrid facilities subject to standardized study deposits, readiness
    requirements, and withdrawal penalties (deter speculative projects).
 5. Hybrid facilities studied based on requested injection and/or withdrawal rights
    (e.g., 500 MW load + 600 MW generation seeking 0 withdrawal / 100 MW injection rights).
 6. Hybrid interconnections must install system-protection facilities to prevent unauthorized
    injections/withdrawals exceeding their rights.
 7. Expedite interconnection study of loads that agree to be curtailable (and dispatchable hybrids);
    operator's curtailment/dispatch control must suffice for operations + planning. Open question:
    can such expedited studies be completed in 60 days?
 8. Load/hybrid facilities responsible for 100% of assigned network upgrades; comment sought on
    whether to offset via a crediting mechanism, and over how many years.
 9. Where the interconnection customer is not the transmission owner, afford the same option-to-build
    as generator interconnection customers.
10. An existing generating facility seeking PARTIAL SUSPENSION to serve a new co-located load must go
    through an SSR/RMR-type study considering system conditions (incl. forecast load growth) at least
    3 years out; suspension only after needed reliability upgrades are in service; upgrade cost on the
    generator. Comment sought on whether/how resource adequacy is considered.
11. Utilities serving large loads (incl. hybrids) responsible for transmission service based on
    withdrawal rights.
12. Utilities serving large loads responsible for ancillary services based on peak demand, without
    consideration of co-located generation; co-located generation fully compensated for ancillary svc.
13. There must be a plan to implement the reforms; comment sought on transition for interconnection
    requests already under study.
14. Utilities serving large loads must meet all applicable NERC reliability standards and OATT
    provisions; NERC should review whether new registration categories / standards are needed for BES
    reliability. (Not intended to discourage utilities from making FPA §205 filings.)
